### [Page] Programme Introduction & Instructions

**Programme Introduction and Information**

Welcome to Certificate Programme in Leadership with AI. The programme helps you apply artificial intelligence, machine learning and leadership to transform businesses. A programme overview video by Professor Anand Nandkumar is embedded on the page.

**Programme Structure**

ISB Certificate Programme in Leadership with AI is a fully online course of 20 weeks, each offering recorded lecture videos, assignments, discussions and playbook activities. The Programme Orientation module provides an overview of the programme along with the programme outline, completion requirements, FAQs and an overview of the learning platform. The learning platform is the central point of access to all programme content.

**Plan your time**
- Each week, on Wednesday, you get access to a new module with video lectures and corresponding assignments.
- Complete all assignments and discussions for the week before the due date — no later than Wednesday, 11:59 PM IST of the following week.
- Office hours are scheduled throughout the programme to interact with programme leaders.

**Prerequisites:** None. All material required is provided via the learning platform.

**Key Activities (Orientation week):** Meet your fellow learners; take the pre-programme survey; read and agree to the Student Code of Conduct and the Course Agreement.

**Completion Requirements:** See the Completion Requirements page for criteria to earn a certificate.

**Intellectual Property (IP) Policy:** Programme materials are the intellectual property of Emeritus and its University Partners. Recording, transcribing, sharing, or distributing programme materials in any manner is expressly prohibited.

**Need Help?**
- Programme Support Team: support.india@emeritus.org (response within 24–48 hours, Mon–Sat).
- Q&A discussions at the end of each module.
- Office hours hosted weekly by the Programme Leader.
- Weekly feedback surveys at the end of each module.

## The Learning Platform

### [Page] Navigating Canvas

Covers Navigating the Programme, Browser Requirements, Creating Your Profile, Managing Assignments, and Managing Discussions.

**Ways to navigate Canvas:** Use the Modules tab (chronological list of all modules; collapse/expand by module); use Previous/Next buttons to move through pages in order.

**Browser requirements:** Works in all major browsers (Chrome, Safari, Firefox, Internet Explorer) on the latest or prior version. Some browsers block non-secure embedded content — use the shield icon in the address bar to allow it.

**Creating your profile:** Account → Profile → Edit Profile; add a bio (company, department, title) and a photo. Current title and role description are required for a complete profile. Web services (LinkedIn, Twitter, Skype) can be linked via Account → Settings → Web Services. Accounts default to IST time zone.

**Managing assignments:** Accessible via Modules tab (conceptual order) and Grades tab (completion status). Dashboard also shows "To do" stream, "Coming up" feed, "Recent feedback" stream, and Calendar.

**Managing discussions:** Select Reply at the bottom of a discussion page, type a response, and Post Reply.

### [Page] Completion Requirements

A verified Digital Certificate of Completion is awarded on successful completion. All activities are marked complete/incomplete — a submission with all key elements receives full points; an incomplete activity receives 0 points. There is at least one required assignment per week counting toward completion; self-study activities are optional.

You must score a minimum of 70% across all required assignments including capstone. Assignment category weights: Required Activities 70%, Capstone 30%.

**Submissions policy:** On-time submissions marked within one week of the due date. Late assignments accepted until one week after the programme end date. Re-submissions allowed within one week of grading only for assignments originally submitted on time. Grading completed within 7–14 days of the due date.
