# Module 3: AI and Managerial Trade-offs (Week 3)

> This week: 9 videos, 1 optional discussion, 1 required assignment (~4–5 hours).

---

### [Page] Module 3: Introduction and Instructions

Professor Anand covers the five levels in developing AI strategy, the need to consider data requirements when trading off interpretability vs. complexity of algorithms, and the limitations and pitfalls of AI.

**Programme-level outcomes addressed this week:**
- Create a roadmap for AI adoption and implementation, considering both short- and long-term success.
- Foster an AI-first culture within the organisation, promoting data-driven decision-making.

**Week learning outcomes:**
- Develop a roadmap for implementing AI in your organisation/division/functional area.
- Compare and contrast different AI methods based on accuracy and complexity.
- Describe the concept of augmented intelligence and how AI can aid humans in better decision-making.
- Recognise the limitations of AI.

**Key activities:** Videos 1–9; Required Assignment 3.2: AI Implementation Roadmap. All to be completed by Wednesday, April 29, 2026, 11:59 IST.

**Self-study:** Optional Discussion 3.1: Failed AI Cases.

## Video Lectures

- **Video 1 (03:04): Module Overview** — How to understand and implement AI in an organisation; challenges organisations face after embracing AI.
- **Video 2 (05:24): The Missing Middle — Augmented Intelligence** — AI and humans working synergistically in the "missing middle" to enhance productivity, innovation and growth.
- **Video 3 (08:27): Harnessing Augmented Intelligence — The Power of Human-AI Collaboration** — "Augmented intelligence" enhancing decision-making, data processing and communication; AI's role in human interactions and physical embodiments.
- **Video 4 (05:39): Developing an AI Strategy** — A five-level roadmap to develop an AI strategy; avoiding pitfalls; developing a business case for AI.
- **Video 5 (10:37): AI Organisational Structure** — Relationship between AI strategy and organisational structure; AI-ready structures considering centralisation, team composition, autonomy, integration; benefits and challenges of flat, tall and matrix structures.
- **Video 6 (07:20): AI Methods — Balancing Complexity, Accuracy and Bias** — Trade-offs in AI methods for managers; reasons for AI inaccuracy; challenges and risks of implementation; biases and how to address them.
- **Video 7 (09:50): Deep Learning — AI's Biases and Limitations** — Challenges of bias, opacity, and correlation vs. causation; integrating human expertise with AI predictions for informed and ethical decision-making.
- **Video 8 (12:10): AI Readiness — Transforming Organisations for Strategic Success** — Not all organisations are AI-ready; successful adoption requires high-quality data, digital transformation, strategic vision, executive leadership, partnerships, and willingness to experiment.
- **Video 9 (01:59): Module Summary** — Key takeaways from the module.

### [Assignment] Required Assignment 3.2: AI Implementation Roadmap

**Points:** 25 | **Due:** April 29, 2026 (11:59 PM IST) | **Submission:** online upload | **Suggested time:** 120 minutes

**Learning outcomes addressed:**
- Develop a roadmap for implementing AI in your organisation/division/functional area.
- Describe the concept of augmented intelligence and how AI can aid humans in better decision-making.

**Instructions:** As a leader/manager with a foundational understanding of ML algorithms, contextualise this knowledge by identifying potential applications within your organisation, enabling it to advance AI maturity systematically.

**Tasks:**
1. **Select two managerial functions** relevant to your organisation that could benefit from AI implementation.
2. **Develop an AI Maturity Model** for each function, progressing through four stages.
3. **Detail activities and steps** for each stage.

**Stage framework:**

- **Awareness Stage** — Education and Training (training programs, resources); Industry Research; Stakeholder Engagement; Risk Assessment.
- **Active Stage** — Pilot Projects; Resource Allocation; Data Preparation; Technology Evaluation; Performance Metrics.
- **Operational Stage** — Infrastructure Development; Process Integration; Skill Development; Governance and Compliance; Scaling Up.
- **Transformational Stage** — Strategic Alignment; Cultural Shift; Ecosystem Engagement; Continuous Improvement; Innovation Pipeline.

**Submission:** Upload a Word document → Start Assignment → upload file → Submit Assignment.
