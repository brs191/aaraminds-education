### [Discussion] Self-Study Discussion 4.1: Case Study — Polaroid

**Learning outcome addressed:** Recognise the ways to harness AI to benefit businesses and enable disruption.

The case study examines why Polaroid — the dominant instant-photography company from the post-WWII era until around 2000 — failed to survive the shift to digital photography. Key themes from the case: failure to capitalise on its own early digital-imaging research; overreliance on the high-margin instant-film business; risk aversion following the costly failure of the Polavision home video system; and a perfectionist culture that delayed product launches until competitors had already captured the digital market.

**Questions for discussion:**
1. What could be the reasons that Polaroid's top management continued to back outdated technologies?
2. What could have gone wrong in Polaroid's market research?
3. How can an organisation capitalise on incremental and disruptive innovations simultaneously?
4. How can an organisation manage risk linked to innovations?

_Source: predictableprofits.com article on Polaroid's collapse. Self-study activity — does not count toward programme completion._
