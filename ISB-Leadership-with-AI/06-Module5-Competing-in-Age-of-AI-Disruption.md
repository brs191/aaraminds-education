### [Page] Module 5: Introduction and Instructions

The module explores servitisation — transforming traditional products into service-based models using data, AI and generative AI. Integrating AI and digital technologies lets businesses unlock new opportunities, reshape value chains, and develop innovative business models. Examples include pay-per-use models and predictive maintenance.

**Programme-level outcomes addressed this week:**
- Understand the technological aspects of AI, including limitations and ethical considerations.
- Develop leadership skills to drive AI initiatives and communicate their value.

**Week learning outcomes:**
- Understand servitisation and its impact on business models through data and AI.
- Recognise the transformative role of generative AI in converting products into services.
- Evaluate the economic opportunities of AI-driven servitisation beyond productivity gains.
- Analyse real-world AI-enabled product-as-a-service models.
- Identify and explain the three core processes in any organisation.
- Analyse how data and AI enhance core processes — increasing cash flows, accelerating revenue, minimising risks.

### [Assignment] Required Assignment 5.2: Servitisation in the Auto Industry

**Points:** 20 | **Due:** May 13, 2026 (11:59 PM IST) | **Submission:** online upload | **Suggested time:** 90 minutes

**Learning outcome addressed:** Understand servitisation and its impact on business models through data and AI.

**Case context (summary):** Servitisation bundles a product with its essential services under usage-based payment — the World Economic Forum's "experience economy." Early examples: Xerox's pay-per-page model (1960s); Rolls-Royce's "Power-by-the-Hour" for aircraft engines (now ~half its revenue). The automotive industry has had limited servitisation success (single-digit revenue share). The case briefs several OEM efforts: Toyota's gamified Kinto subscription (2019); Volvo's "Care by Volvo" flat-fee program (2017) and its insurance lessons; subscription-based vehicle features (BMW heated seats, plus VW, Audi, Toyota, Tesla); Mercedes-Benz "Collection" pilot (2018, suspended after two years but yielded valuable customer data); Jaguar Land Rover's "Pivotal" / 'Own. Subscribe. Rent.' proposition; Ford's sale of its "Canvas" subscription service; and Maruti Suzuki's "Subscribe" offering in India.

**Questions to address in a CEO-ready report:**
1. How is digital servitisation expected to impact the automotive industry — changes in business models, customer relationships, service offerings?
2. What is AI's role in the digital servitisation journey in the Indian automotive market — which AI applications/technologies will drive it?
3. What digital technologies, AI systems and data infrastructure are necessary for digital servitisation in the automotive sector, and why are they crucial?
4. Apply the 5V Business Model (Value Creation, Value Delivery, Value Appropriation) to Maruti's subscription model. Assess whether Maruti needs to build new capabilities or transform existing ones.
