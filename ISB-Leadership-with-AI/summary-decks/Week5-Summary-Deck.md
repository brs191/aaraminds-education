Value Delivery
--- Page 14 ---
Porter's Value Chain Model — Primary Activities: inbound logistics, operations, outbound logistics, marketing and sales, services. Secondary Activities: procurement, infrastructure, human resource management, technological development. (Source: Porter M. E., Competitive Advantage, Free Press, 1985, p.37.) Raw materials, components, equipment and services; R&D, product design, market research and process development; recruitment, hiring and training; company overhead, management, financing and planning.
--- Page 15 ---
Traditional vs Transformed Business — Inbound logistics / Operations: essential to integrate RFID and digital platforms; data platform setup crucial. Outbound logistics / Marketing & sales: D2C now critical, requires new warehousing and inventory management; price the experience, not just the product; new algorithms and data centres required; focus on data collection, storage, analysis; target B2C and B2B with distinct strategies.
--- Page 16 ---
Traditional vs Transformed Business — After-sales service: data-driven, proactive issue resolution. Stakeholder management: more complex, varies between B2C and B2B. Infrastructure / Technological development: includes both digital and physical components; digital capabilities for data management and predictive modeling essential; ambidextrous skills development crucial.

Transforming Core Business Processes with Data and AI
--- Page 18 ---
Defining the Core Processes — Are critical for any organisation or sector; essential for delivering value to customers; can be managed internally or outsourced. Involvement in these processes is essential for organisational success.
--- Page 19 ---
Core Business Processes — Product Development: understanding customer needs, developing solutions, coordinating design. Supply Chain: selecting suppliers, managing logistics, handling customer installations. Customer Relationship: identifying new customers, executing marketing programmes, enhancing loyalty. Managing and integrating multiple channels reduces conflicts and risks. The goal is to enhance trust and loyalty for long-term customer retention.
--- Page 20 ---
Impact of Core Processes — Product development. Accelerating cash flow: speed up development cycles, use new technologies for faster market entry. Increasing cash flow: differentiate products to boost market appeal, increase cash flow with unique offerings. Minimising risk: innovate quickly to stay ahead, lower obsolescence risk with continuous improvement. Direct impact on firm/shareholder value.
--- Page 21 ---
Impact of Core Processes — Supply chain management. Accelerating cash flow: speed up adoption by channels and OEMs, boost cash flow with faster integration. Increasing cash flow: reduce working capital with JIT, free up cash by optimising inventory. Minimising risk: raise switching costs for suppliers, strengthen supply chain stability.
--- Page 22 ---
Impact of Core Processes — Customer relationship management. Accelerating cash flow: use a large customer base for quicker product launches, speed up time to market. Increasing cash flow: provide superior service and high-margin products, increase revenue with better offerings. Minimising risk: lower acquisition costs and boost retention, raise switching costs for customer loyalty.

Coda Coffee's Supply Chain Transformation with AI and Data
--- Page 26 ---
Benefits of Using Technology in Supply Chain — Integration of technologies enhances the supply chain by achieving transparency and efficiency, benefiting the business and its customers.
--- Page 27 ---
Convergence of AI and Data — Digital technologies capture and generate data. AI brings everything together and fuels the entire supply chain. AI helps integrate and analyse vast amounts of data.
--- Page 28 ---
Coda Coffee: History and Ethics — Vision of a coffee company with a social mission. Aims to be socially responsible and financially sustainable. Focuses on sustainability by sourcing coffee beans from developing countries. Founded by brothers Tim and Tom. Ensures an ethical supply chain with fair-trade payments directly to farmers. Certified by organisations to guarantee sustainable practices. Faced challenges with ensuring consistent bean quality. Responded to customer demand for high-quality beans.
--- Page 29 ---
Coda Coffee: Overcoming Challenges — Introduced Farm to Cup Programme. Trained farmers in sustainable farming practices. Certified farmers for high-quality products. Supported farmers' financial stability and promoted sustainable farming. Established a direct relationship with farmers, eliminating intermediaries and ensuring transparency.
--- Page 30 ---
Coda Coffee: Collaboration with Bext360 — To reconfigure and reimagine their supply chain, Coda Coffee partnered with Bext360. Maintained the quality of coffee beans and the ethics. Enabled real-time payments to farmers and provided feedback. Used AI, blockchain and IoT to enhance transparency in supply chain. Benefitted from an AI-based solution to determine the quality of minerals.
--- Page 31 ---
AI-Powered Project: Uganda's Green Coffee Milling Machine — Vision: provided visual perception for assessing the quality of coffee beans. AI: handled computational aspects. Blockchain: ensured food security, records of sustainability and fair-trade practices. IoT: monitored coffee cherry deposition and quality; collected data on cherry quality and stored it on the blockchain.
