Origin and Evolution of Generative AI
--- Page 3 ---
Origin of Large Language Models — Based on Hidden Markov models, where unobserved states influence future states. Intuitive interfaces like ChatGPT democratised AI use, making it accessible to a broader audience. LLMs derive models from vast data, building theories without prior knowledge.
--- Page 4 ---
Statistical Learning in Language Models — Statistical learning reveals structured relationships in data. Hidden Markov models predicted the next word in a sequence.
--- Page 5 ---
Traditional Language Models: Challenges — Exploding Gradients (lead to instability and bizarre results). Memory Loss (affects recall of earlier sentence parts and later predictions).
--- Page 6 ---
Key Features of Transformers — Language data forms a time series represented as a matrix. Predictions based on correlational structure in the matrix. Matrix helps predict the next word in a sequence.
--- Page 7 ---
Unimodal vs Multimodal Language Models — Unimodal: process and generate one type of input and output (e.g., text-to-text). Multimodal: handle multiple input and output types (e.g., image-to-text, text-to-music). The models' versatility is demonstrated through their ability to produce text, images, and music.
--- Page 8 ---
Training Methods for Language Models — Some models trained on preceding text only. Encoder-decoder models consider past and future context. Bidirectional approach enhances prediction accuracy and relevance.
--- Page 9 ---
Advantages of Large Language Models — Large models improve performance with more data. Smaller models plateau as they reach data saturation.

Google's AI Pivot: From Missed Chances to Gemini
--- Page 11 ---
ChatGPT's Disruption — Conversational interface: ChatGPT's user-friendly design made it highly accessible for everyday use. Disruptive force: the democratisation of technology, making it accessible to a wider audience.
--- Page 12 ---
ChatGPT's Rise: Google's Missed AI Opportunity — Polaroid: failed to commercialise digital printing. Google: was slow to enter the conversational AI market; allowed OpenAI and Microsoft to take the lead.
--- Page 13 ---
Google's Reasons for Its Failure — Internal Resistance and Complacency: Google's CEO Sundar Pichai admitted complacency led to significant losses. Business Model Challenge: the conversational interface conflicted with Google's ad-dependent search engine model. Revenue Model Integration: integrating traditional ad-based revenue models into a conversational interface posed a significant challenge.
--- Page 14 ---
Google's 'Code Red': A Response to AI Competitors — 'Code Red' Declaration: Google declared a 'Code Red' to reassess its AI strategy. Founders' Involvement: the urgency rallied Google's founders. Competitive Threat: competitors described as 'Pirates,' threatening the existing order. AI Arms Race: industry giants are fully engaged in the AI arms race.
--- Page 15 ---
Google's Strategic Acquisition — Google is working with DeepMind and Google Brain to foster innovative breakthroughs. Complex Applications: emphasis on complex decision-making applications, combining predictive capabilities of models like AlphaGo with the versatility of LLMs. Broad AI Focus: Google's focus extends beyond generative AI and LLMs.

AI Investments: Tech-Centric vs Customer-Centric Approaches
--- Page 17 ---
AI Investment Strategies — Maximise potential through diverse AI opportunities. Adopt technocentric strategy focusing on generative AI capabilities.
--- Page 18 ---
Technocentric AI Investment Model — Layered perspective evaluates AI opportunities. Base layer: hardware, including semiconductor industry investments. Infrastructure stack: cloud services and LLM models. Development and engineering: platforms for AI application deployment. Top layer: end-user applications like content generation tools. Requires massive capital investments, supported at the national level as a strategic sector. Investments range from millions to billions; development from millions; applications from thousands to millions.
--- Page 19 ---
Transformative Impact of LLMs on App Development — LLMs revolutionise app development. Tasks that once took 6-8 months can now be completed in days or even hours. Rapid development enables faster assembly and testing of apps.
--- Page 20 ---
Customer-Centric Framework — Leverage generative AI, emerging technologies and data for new business models. Focus on creating, capturing and delivering value through innovation.
--- Page 21 ---
Transforming Supply Chains with Generative AI and Business Models — Customer-facing supply chain requires more than choosing an AI stack layer. Leveraging generative AI and data to solve major problems is crucial. Solving these problems paves the way for new business models and revenue opportunities. Success depends on monetisation and the business model, not just the technology.
