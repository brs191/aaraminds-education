# Who Owns MCP Governance?

## Deliverable Type

- Leadership-oriented LinkedIn article
- Operating model discussion

## Core Themes

- Platform ownership
- AI governance
- Security vs platform responsibilities
- Centralized vs federated governance
- Runtime accountability
- Operational discipline

## Core Thesis

Most enterprises have unclear ownership for MCP systems.

Responsibility is fragmented across AI teams, platform engineering, security, architecture governance, DevOps, and compliance.

Without clear ownership, governance becomes inconsistent, approvals become ambiguous, and runtime risk expands silently.

## Narrative Angle

This post should speak to leaders. The argument is that MCP governance cannot sit entirely with security, AI engineering, platform teams, or architecture governance alone. It needs a federated operating model with clear decision rights.

## Strategic Purpose

Drive executive engagement, governance discussion, and operational maturity thinking.

## Suggested CTA

The first governance question is not technical. It is: who is accountable when an agent acts through an MCP server?
