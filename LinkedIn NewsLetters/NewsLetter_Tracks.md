## The filter

Every topic on this list passes three tests:

1. **Edge test.** Draws on something only Raja can credibly say — Samsung Principal Engineer (17 yrs Korea), AT&T Associate Director (60+ engineering org, AI-assisted engineering adoption, 30% GTM acceleration), eSIM/RCS commercialization, 35% cloud-cost optimization via FinOps, regulated enterprise default.
2. **Audience test.** Speaks to senior tech leaders, AI engineering leaders, enterprise architects, GCC executives, and the VP-hiring crowd — not to AI generalists or builders.
3. **Decision test.** Gives the reader a decision lens, not a news take. AaraMinds is *clarity, courage, context* — not commentary.

Topics that don't pass all three are off the list, even if they'd get engagement.

## How to run this as a system

**Rotation, not random selection.** Two newsletters per month (current biweekly cadence). One from Lane 1, the other alternating between Lane 2 and Lane 3. The rotation prevents one-lane fatigue and trains the audience to expect breadth at a consistent altitude.

**LinkedIn posts as probes for newsletters.** The Lane 2 post on "who decides what AI is safe enough" — if it pulls hard, that's the next Lane 2 newsletter. Don't commit a newsletter to a thesis that hasn't been pressure-tested with a 90-second LinkedIn read first.

**Re-use, don't re-invent.** Every newsletter should yield two or three LinkedIn extracts and one Featured-section artifact for the LinkedIn profile (closes the loop on the VP-search positioning play — see profile gaps identified separately).
