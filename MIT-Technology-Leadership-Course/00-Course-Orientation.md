## Course Introduction and Information *(Page)*

**Course Introduction**

Welcome to MIT xPRO's Advanced Program in Technology Leadership & Innovation course. We are glad to have you in the course and look forward to helping you build technology leadership skills to enable technological innovation and strategy.

**Course Structure**

The Advanced Program in Technology Leadership & Innovation is a fully online course with 36 weeks, each offering recorded lecture videos, activities, resources, and live sessions.

The Course Orientation week provides an overview of the course along with the syllabus, grading and assessment details, FAQs, and an overview of the learning platform. The learning platform is the central point of access to all course content including video lectures, assignments, and discussions.

Plan Your Time:

- Each week, on Wednesday, you gain access to a new week with video lectures and corresponding assignments.
- Complete all assignments, discussions, and quizzes for the week before the due date, which is no later than Thursday at 11:59 PM IST of the following week.
- Live sessions (webinars) and office hours are scheduled throughout the course to help you interact with faculty and course leaders.

**Key Activities**

Each week you are assigned required activities and self-study activities. During Course Orientation week you must: ensure prerequisites are complete; meet your fellow learners; take the pre-course survey; read and agree to the Student Code of Conduct and the Course Agreement.

**Completion Requirements**

See the Completion Requirements page for the criteria required to successfully complete the course and earn a certificate of completion.

**Live Sessions**

Through live sessions you can interact with your Learning Facilitator and ask content-related questions. Select the Live Sessions tab on the left navigation pane to view the schedule.

**System Requirements**

See the System Requirements and Tools page to learn the system setup required for the course.

**Intellectual Property**

Course materials are the intellectual property of Emeritus and its University Partners. Recording, transcribing, sharing, or distributing course materials in any manner is expressly prohibited.

**Need Help?**

- Help Center — select Support in the course menu.
- Support Request Form — select Submit Request at the top of the Help Center page.
- Q&A Discussions — at the end of each week, post questions on the Q&A discussion boards.
- Office Hours — your Learning Facilitator hosts office hours every week.
- Weekly Surveys — share feedback in the weekly surveys.

## Navigating Canvas *(Page)*

Ways to Navigate Canvas:

- **Use the Modules tab** — select Modules in the left navigation pane to view the list of weeks for the entire program, with each page and activity listed chronologically. Weeks can be collapsed/expanded.
- **Use the Progress Bar within the Modules** — each page has a progress bar of icons under the page title. Your current location is indicated by a pin icon and shading. Completed items turn green with a checkmark.
- **Use Previous and Next Buttons** — progress through pages in order with Next; return with Previous.

Internet Browsers: The platform works well in all major browsers (Chrome, Safari, Firefox, Internet Explorer) on the latest version.

Browser Security: Different browsers sometimes block non-secure content embedded in a secure website. If embedded content (e.g., video) does not appear, click the shield icon in the address bar and confirm you would like to view the content.

Creating Your Profile: Select Account → Profile → Edit Profile. Enter your bio (include company, department, title). Upload a picture. Save Profile. Current title and role description are required for a complete profile; a photo is strongly recommended. You can also link social media (LinkedIn, Twitter, Skype) via Account → Settings → Web Services.

Note: By default, all accounts are set to the UTC time zone. Notification emails and assignment due dates display in UTC unless changed.

Managing Assignments: Access assignments from the Modules tab (in intended order) and the Grades tab (lists all required components and completion status). The dashboard also shows a "To do" stream, "Coming up" feed, "Recent feedback" stream, and Calendar.

Managing Discussions: Select Reply at the bottom of the discussion page, type your response, and select Post Reply. Making the first line a brief subject statement is encouraged.

## Completion Requirements *(Page)*

Upon successful completion you receive a verified Digital Certificate of Participation from MIT xPRO. To complete the course and receive a certificate you must score a minimum of **75%**. For each Capstone Component, you must attempt each criterion of the Capstone assignment.

Grade category weights:

| Category | Description | Weight |
|---|---|---|
| Category 1: Assignments | Assignments in Modules 1–23 | 20% |
| Category 2: Discussions | Discussions in Modules 1–23 | 20% |
| Category 3: Workbook Activities | Workbook activities and their submission area | 30% |
| Category 4: Capstone Components | All required capstone components | 30% |

If a submission includes all key elements outlined in an activity, it receives full points; an incomplete activity receives 0 points. Self-study activities (prefix "Self-Study") are optional and do not count toward completion. All end-of-week workbooks are graded complete/incomplete.

Assignment Submission Policy:

- **On time** — graded within one week of the due date; some assignments receive written comments within one to two weeks.
- **Late** — accepted until one week after the course end date; graded but no written comments.
- **Re-submissions** — assignments originally submitted on time and marked incomplete or scoring under 50% may be resubmitted within one week of assessment. Assignments originally submitted late are not eligible for re-submission.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs) *(Page)*

**Accessing the Course Content** — A new week is released every Thursday with an email from Program Support. The course outline is a separate page in Course Orientation. All needed resources are provided via the platform. There are no examinations, but required assignments/activities are mandatory for the certificate. You have seven days to complete activities once released. Recommended commitment: 4–6 hours per week. Support is available via Learning Facilitators (Canvas email) and Program Support (technical). Content is rolled out in a specific sequence by design. Course materials are copyrighted by/licensed to MIT xPRO; participants may make a reasonable number of machine-readable copies only for personal, non-commercial use. Course material remains accessible for 12 months from the course end date. Extensions are available on request via Program Support. The platform is accessible during the holiday break (Program Support unavailable).

**Accessing the Course Completion Certificate** — A digital smart certificate is issued upon successful completion, within 6–8 weeks (45–60 business days) of the program end date. Passing criteria are on the Completion Requirements page. Certificates are only issued to those who successfully complete requirements. Refund schedule: full refund within 15 days of commencement; 50% from 16–30 days; no refund after 30 days. Cancellations via admissions@emeritus.org or the Support tab. Deferrals are available subject to conditions and fees (deferral requests to admissions@emeritus.org).

**Navigating the Learning Platform** — Tutorials are available for new users. The course can be accessed from desktop, laptop, tablet, or mobile (Emeritus Classroom App for Android/iOS).

**Participating in the Course** — Submit assignments via the Start Assignment button (upload a document and/or enter text), then Submit Assignment. Contact your Learning Facilitator or classmates via the Canvas Inbox or discussion boards. Course Leaders, Learning Facilitators, and staff may not share participant work outside the platform; exemplary work is shared only with consent.

**Customizing the Learning Platform** — Edit your profile via Account. Set notification preferences for new content and discussion posts. Change the default UTC time zone via Account → Settings → Edit Settings → select time zone → Update Settings.
