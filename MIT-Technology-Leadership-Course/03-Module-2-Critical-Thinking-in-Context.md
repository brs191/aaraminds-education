## Information Literacy

### Introduction to Information Literacy *(Page)*

A modern skill important for critical thinking is "information literacy" — how someone evaluates content in traditional media sources. According to Jonathan Haber (author of *Critical Thinking*), information literacy is the ability to identify what information is needed, understand how information is organized, identify the best sources, locate them, evaluate them critically, and share that information.

The information-literate apply these steps:

- **Locating information** — finding the highest-quality sources using diverse techniques beyond simple web searches.
- **Evaluating information** — applying tests to analyze information for accuracy, relevance, and timeliness.
- **Organizing information** — bringing order to collections of information to reveal patterns or make it more useful for a task.
- **Synthesizing information** — using information gathered, evaluated, and organized to accomplish tasks such as answering questions or creating work products.
- **Communicating information** — sharing what has been created so the knowledge becomes part of an information ecosystem.

Haber (2020): whether background knowledge comes from print or digital sources, or from years of study or quick research, the bottom line for critical thinking is that you cannot think critically about a subject you do not understand.

*Reference: Haber, J. (2020). Critical Thinking. The MIT Press Essential Knowledge Series.*
