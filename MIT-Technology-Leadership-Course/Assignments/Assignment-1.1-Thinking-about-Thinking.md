## Instructions

In this assignment, you'll explore a real-world scenario that focuses on understanding how you form your conclusion to an argument.

Academics and managers have long believed that organizations had to make trade-offs between flexibility and efficiency. A central notion of the academic theory on organizational design is contingency — the idea that organizations and their associated processes need to be designed to match the nature of the work they carry out. One of the most common variables in contingency theory is the degree of uncertainty in the surrounding environment (often also conceptualized as the need for innovation). When both the competitive environment and the associated work are stable and well understood, contingency theory suggests that organizations need more highly structured, mechanistic designs. In contrast, when facing highly uncertain situations that require ongoing adaptation, the theory suggests that organizations will perform better with more flexible, organic designs.

Due to the COVID-19 pandemic, every organization — whether identified as a highly structured or flexible environment — has had to adapt to a new way of working remotely/online. Some organizations claim they can safely bring back employees to work, while others say it's impossible to guarantee their safety.

In 400 to 500 words, answer these three questions (consider the evaluation criteria when completing your assignment):

1. Which side of the argument do you think is right, and why?
2. What evidence supports your claims?
3. How did you come to a conclusion?

*Reference: D. Keiffer, N. P. Repenning, and J. Repenning (2018). A new approach to designing work.*
