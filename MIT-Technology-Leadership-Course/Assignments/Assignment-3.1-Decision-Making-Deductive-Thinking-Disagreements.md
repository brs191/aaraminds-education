## Instructions

In this assignment, you'll explore real-world scenarios that focus on decision making, deductive thinking, and disagreements.

### Scenario 1

Imagine working in an organization where the senior director has four direct reports. One of the units reporting to the senior director is five times the size of the others and is composed of three equally sized units. The director of this large entity is ready to retire; therefore, one of your colleagues has proposed that instead of conducting a search for a new director, you could divide up the large unit so that the managers of the three sub-parts of the largest unit would each report to the senior director. If this were to happen, there would be six direct reports, which is still manageable, and the organization will save money because the salary of the director of the large unit will not have to be paid after retirement.

**Question 1:** Analyze the argument. Using deductive thinking, identify the unstated premises that should be articulated and evaluated. Create a counter argument with possible reasons to keep the organizational structure in its current form.

### Scenario 2

Provost is one of North America's most classic and iconic bike manufacturers. They produce a wide variety of high-quality commuter bicycles, mountain bikes, and electric bikes. The firm has two options for manufacturing its latest product. One option is to choose a substantial plant that will make all the bicycles in one location and can ship them to the various retailers worldwide where they will be sold. The other is to have five different plants, each of which are closer to the eventual points of sale. A colleague argues for the second option for three reasons: shipping costs will be reduced; there will be more commitment from customers because the nearby plants support the local economy; and the company will be more resilient to disruptions because one event is less likely to close down five different plants in five different places.

**Question 2:** You can understand the points of the second option, but you aren't convinced. What evidence would be needed to validate the argument of picking the second option? However, if you feel strongly that the first option is the better choice, argue your points and explain your reasoning.
