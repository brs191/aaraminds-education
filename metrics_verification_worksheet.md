# Metrics Verification Worksheet

**Purpose:** Every headline number in your plans is load-bearing — the same six metrics appear across all five company plans. If one is soft when probed, the whole metrics-led narrative wobbles. Fill this in honestly. A defensible smaller number beats an impressive one you can't back up.

**How to use:** For each metric, complete all columns. Then take the "Defensibility self-test" at the bottom. Anything you can't answer in one breath is a number to soften, re-scope, or drop.

---

## The drill for every metric

For each one, you must be able to say, in ~30 seconds and without hesitation:
1. **Baseline** — what was the starting number, and how was it measured?
2. **Result** — the end number, same measurement method.
3. **Timeframe** — over what period, and when?
4. **Your role** — "I" vs "we": what did *you* personally own vs. the team/org?
5. **Method** — how was the delta measured (tool, dashboard, finance report, estimate)?
6. **Attribution** — how do you know your work caused it, not a confound?

## Defensibility self-test

For each metric, rate honestly:
- 🟢 **Rock-solid** — exact numbers, my ownership clear, can defend the method under follow-ups.
- 🟡 **Directional** — roughly right but the method/baseline is soft. → Soften the language ("meaningfully reduced," "roughly a third").
- 🔴 **Can't defend** — don't remember baseline, was mostly team work, or it was an estimate. → Drop it or reframe as a qualitative story.

**Rule:** Never lead with a 🔴 number. A bar-raiser will pull exactly one thread to test whether your metrics are real — make sure every thread you offer holds.

---

## Quick reference: which metric to lead with, by panel
- **Engineering panel:** #3 (performance), #1 (scale) — but only the precise versions.
- **Hiring manager / Director readiness:** #5 (org), #4 (predictability).
- **FinOps / business judgment:** #2 (cost).
- **AI strategy:** #7 — and be ruthless about POC-vs-production honesty.
- **Global / stakeholder:** #6 (eSIM).
